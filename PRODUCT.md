# Product

## Register

product

## Users

AgentWeb's primary users are developers who want coding agents to use mapped
websites without repeating browser work. The local analytics dashboard is for the
AgentWeb maintainer, who needs to understand adoption, reliability, and retention
quickly without inspecting raw event logs.

## Product Purpose

AgentWeb turns mapped website actions into predictable commands that agents can
discover and run. The analytics surface shows whether strangers install it,
complete useful work, return, and encounter failures. Success means the maintainer
can decide what to fix or build next within seconds.

## Brand Personality

Direct, capable, and honest. AgentWeb should feel like a dependable developer tool,
not a marketing dashboard. It explains what is known, what failed, and what data is
or is not collected.

## Anti-references

Do not resemble a sprawling enterprise analytics suite, a generic collection of
identical metric cards, or a decorative dark observability dashboard. Avoid vanity
metrics without context, hidden definitions, and interfaces that require setup
before showing a useful local view.

## Design Principles

- Answer the important question first, then expose supporting detail.
- Make every metric's meaning and time window explicit.
- Prefer honest empty states and errors to invented or stale data.
- Keep normal workflows prompt-first while making underlying mechanics inspectable.
- Protect user trust by never collecting prompts, arguments, outputs, URLs, or credentials.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. All controls must work from a keyboard, status must not rely on
color alone, focus must be visible, motion must respect reduced-motion preferences,
and dense data must remain legible at narrow desktop and mobile widths.
